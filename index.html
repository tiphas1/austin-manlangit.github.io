CONSULTING ISSUE

Collaboration is neverending in people's everyday lives. People experience collaboration at home when talking to mom about what to do for dinner. In school, people collaborate with our colleagues with group studying and working together on projects. At work, people collaborate with their teams, brainstorming ideas, bringing different perspectives, and providing solutions to solve problems. Collaboration in the workplace is beneficial for many reasons, the most important being that it brings people together to share knowledge that promotes innovation and creativity when it comes to solving a problem or starting and completing a new task. When teams and people from different departments or companies collaborate, they are all able to utilize on the strengths and skills of one another in which people are able to share knowledge with each other. With Modern Solution, we have been approached with the question on what are some factors affect collaboration in the modern work environment?

  • Transformational Leadership

    Leading by example

    Employees prefer leaders who help them stay productive through inspiring, engaging, and learning from them

  • Employee Autonomy

    Make your own decisions

    Leaders who let their colleagues make their on decisions on how their work will be done brings task efficiency and innovation

  • Proactive Performance

    Show the initiative

    Leaders who encourage proactive problem-solving and initiative influence employees to work harder and efficiently

  • Work Process Efficiency

    Create efficient workflows

    Leaders are able to identify what needs to be done and focus on creating and improve workflows to make processes more efficient

  • Organizational Commitment

    Do you like it here?

    Employees who exhibited high organizational commitment believed their workplace offered transformational leadership, have more job autonomy, proactively performed, and were efficient within their job processes

Alternative Methods

Human Hemoglobin Denaturation
Using human hemoglobin to study protein denaturation caused by chemicals is one valid method that could replace the Draize test. Protein denaturation is one of the most important factors that causes corneal opacity. Corneal opacity indicates scarring of the cornea, a direct sign of eye irritation. Different known irritants and non-irritants compounds were applied to the hemoglobin and studied. Hemoglobin denaturation was determined by comparing absorbance of treated and untreated wells by measuring the peak of maximum absorption. Decreased absorption indicated that the compound was an irritant. This method was able to differentiate between irritant and non-irritants and provide enough information about the potnential corneal damage of different compounds. You can read more about human hemoglobin denaturation here.
Reconstituted Human Corneal Epithelium
This 3D epithelial model was cultivated from human corneal cells and could be a promising method for the predication of eye irritation caused by irritatnts. In a study, 65 cosmetic products and 36 chemical solutions were tested using a simplified toxicokinatic approach. This model was able to predict human ocular response and the data collected from this model correlated with the respective scores in historic Draize tests. You can read more about the reconstituted human corneal epithelium model here.
Isolated Chicken Eye Test
The ICE test uses chicken eyes collected from slaughter houses. 44 chemical compounds were used to study the effects of eye irritation on the chicken eyes. Categories were made to organize the data collected; corneal swelling, corneal opacity, fluorescein retantion of damaged corneal epithelium, and an index score which was a combination of the three. categories. The data collected from this study correlated positively with the past Draize test scores. It was concluded that the ICE test can be a prescreen for the Draize test. You can read more about the isolated chicken eye test here.
Short Time Exposure Test
The STE test is a cytotoxicity test using saline or mineral oil as the test solvent. This test uses rabbit corneal cell line cells and uses cell viabiity at a constant concentration as the endpoint for irritation potential. In this study, 51 chemical solutions were tested and data was collected and examined. The STE test was able to predict the eye irritation potential in 5% test concentration. You can read more about the short time exposure test here.
